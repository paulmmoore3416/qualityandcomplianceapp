## 3. Risk Analysis

### 3.1 Severity Scale (S)

| Level | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **1** | Negligible | Temporary discomfort |
| **2** | Minor | Minor injury, temporary impairment |
| **3** | Moderate | Non-permanent injury requiring medical intervention |
| **4** | Serious | Permanent impairment or life-threatening injury |
| **5** | Catastrophic | Death |

### 3.2 Probability Scale (P)

| Level | Description | Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **1** | Rare | < 0.001% (< 1 in 100,000) |
| **2** | Unlikely | 0.001% - 0.01% (1 in 10,000 to 100,000) |
| **3** | Possible | 0.01% - 0.1% (1 in 1,000 to 10,000) |
| **4** | Probable | 0.1% - 1% (1 in 100 to 1,000) |
| **5** | Frequent | > 1% (> 1 in 100) |

### 3.3 Risk Matrix

| S / P | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|---|
| **5** | 5 (M) | 10 (H) | 15 (H) | 20 (C) | 25 (C) |
| **4** | 4 (M) | 8 (M) | 12 (H) | 16 (C) | 20 (C) |
| **3** | 3 (L) | 6 (M) | 9 (H) | 12 (H) | 15 (H) |
| **2** | 2 (L) | 4 (M) | 6 (M) | 8 (M) | 10 (H) |
| **1** | 1 (L) | 2 (L) | 3 (L) | 4 (M) | 5 (M) |

*L = Low, M = Medium, H = High, C = Critical*
